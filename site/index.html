Open Source Novel is a collaborative writing project.
Let’s use open source for more than just software.

What is this?

In November of 2006, I wrote a novel for National Novel Writing Month. in 30 days. You can read more about the story on the Open Source Novel Project Page. After finishing it, I put it in a drawer for just over 6 years.

Recently, I decided to conduct an experiment: To gather a group of contributors to the novel, release it as a collective writing project, and donate the proceeds to charity.

Why?

This project has several reasons for existing. Among them:

  • Some amazing things have been created through open source collaboration. If it works for code, why can’t it work for other arts?
  • This story, though hastily written, has promise. Through the combined efforts of strangers, this could grow into something worth contributing to and, ultimately, reading: the first open source novel ever written.

What can I do?

Glad you asked. Go ahead and check out the issue tracker on github to see what needs to be done. It is updated frequently with opportunities for contribution. Even the littlest bit helps. After all, Steve Klabnik, prolific contributor to Rails, said it well:

Open source is just thousands upon thousands of little tiny pull requests.
